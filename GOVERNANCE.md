# Governance

*Amendment process for Limes Constitution v0.3.*

This constitution is a public working document. It should change through visible reasoning, review, and evaluation links rather than quiet rewrites.

## Versioning

- Use public versions such as `v0.1`, `v0.2`, and `v0.3` while Limes is organizing capability and before any claim of stable deployment governance.
- Increment the minor version for normative changes that affect priorities, release principles, legal-policy framing, evaluation expectations, or assistant behavior.
- Editorial fixes may be merged without a version change when they do not alter meaning.
- Each version should update `CONSTITUTION.md`, `README.md`, and `CHANGELOG.md`.

## Amendment proposals

Substantive pull requests should include:

- Problem statement: what failure mode, ambiguity, or missing obligation is addressed?
- Affected sections: which constitution sections change?
- Rationale: what evidence or argument supports the change?
- Legal/source review: which primary sources or official references were checked, if law or policy is discussed?
- Similarity review: why the text is independent and not a close paraphrase of Anthropic or another constitution.
- Evaluation impact: what EuroBench task, red-team prompt, release checklist item, or model-card field should change?
- Risk assessment: could this cause over-refusal, under-refusal, legal confusion, exclusion of language communities, or open-source chilling effects?

## Reviewers to invite

Depending on the change, invite review from:

- AI safety and evaluation contributors.
- European law and policy readers, especially for AI Act, GDPR, fundamental-rights, or procurement language.
- Public-sector practitioners.
- Civil society or digital-rights reviewers.
- Open-source maintainers and model-card authors.
- Multilingual reviewers for affected languages or regions.

Review should not imply formal endorsement or legal advice. It improves public reasoning.

## Decision posture

Maintainers should prefer changes that:

- Make behavior more testable.
- Improve honesty about limitations.
- Strengthen public accountability and auditability.
- Clarify release controls without weakening open defaults.
- Improve multilingual usefulness and rights awareness.

Maintainers should resist changes that:

- Overclaim Limes capability, legal certainty, compliance, safety, or deployment maturity.
- Add impressive language without operational consequences.
- Collapse open weights, open source, open evaluation, and auditability into one vague claim.
- Closely track another lab's prose or framing.
- Treat European sovereignty as hostility toward non-European people, researchers, or technology.

## Recording decisions

Accepted amendments should update the document history and changelog. Rejected or deferred amendments should receive a brief public explanation when practical.

If maintainers disagree, record the disagreement and the evidence needed to resolve it. A constitution for open European AI should be inspectable in how it changes, not only in what it says.
