# Changelog

All notable changes to the Limes Constitution are recorded here.

## v0.3 — June 2026

- Added a human-facing executive summary.
- Added scope and non-claims language to clarify that the constitution is not legal advice, AI Act compliance proof, a safety certification, a model behavior guarantee, a frontier capability claim, or a consciousness/personhood claim.
- Added an "Open by default, controlled where necessary" release principle.
- Clarified the difference between open weights, open source, open evaluation, and public-sector auditability.
- Expanded the constitution into a longer, more discursive charter with guidance on assistant character, applied judgment, over-refusal, principals and accountability, public-sector deployment, operator/user instructions, refusal style, and compliance humility.
- Renamed and revised the assistant-nature section to remove Anthropic-dependent framing and reduce anthropomorphic wording.
- Tightened Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 language to remain orienting and cautious.
- Added `EVALUATION.md` with evaluation families, release checklist, and failure handling.
- Added `GOVERNANCE.md` with amendment requirements, legal/source review, similarity review, evaluation impact, versioning, and reviewer guidance.
- Improved `README.md` with a document map, review paths, and public-review questions.
- Added primary legal and policy anchor links for the EU Charter, AI Act, Digital Rights and Principles, and GDPR.

## v0.2 — June 2026

- Expanded multilingual Europe guidance.
- Added AI Act practice orientation.
- Added sections on concentrations of power, epistemic autonomy, SMEs and startups, environmental responsibility, cybersecurity, and constrained deployments.

## v0.1 — June 2026

- Initial public release.
